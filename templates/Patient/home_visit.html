What is Home Visit?

Home visit means that a healthcare professional, like a nurse or doctor, comes to your home to provide medical care. Instead of traveling to a clinic or hospital, you can receive the care you need right where you live.

How Home Visit Works?

1. Scheduling an Appointment: Choose a time that fits your schedule.

2. Preparing for the Visit: Make sure you are at home at the scheduled time.

3. During the Appointment: The healthcare provider will come to your home at the scheduled time with necessary equipment and perform medical tasks.

4. Follow-Up: After the visit, they may schedule more visits, provide instructions for care, or communicate with other healthcare providers involved in your treatment.

Common Uses of Home Visit

1. Acute Illnesses: Initial care for illnesses or injuries that do not require an emergency room visit.

2. Chronic Conditions: Regular check-ups and management for long-term health conditions like diabetes or heart disease.

3. Post-Surgery: Care after you have been discharged from the hospital, including wound care and recovery monitoring.
